NAME
    seq - print a sequence of numbers

WHAT IT DOES
    seq prints a list of numbers in order, one per line. You tell it
    where to start, where to stop, and how much to count by. It works
    with both whole numbers and decimals.

    This is great for creating loops in scripts or generating test data.

HOW TO USE IT
    seq LAST
    seq FIRST LAST
    seq FIRST INCREMENT LAST

    With one argument, seq counts from 1 to LAST.
    With two arguments, it counts from FIRST to LAST by 1.
    With three arguments, it counts from FIRST to LAST by INCREMENT.

OPTIONS
    -s SEP, --separator=SEP
        Use SEP between numbers instead of a newline.
        Default is a newline.

    -w, --equal-width
        Pad all numbers with leading zeros so they are all the same
        width. For example: 01, 02, 03 ... 10.

    -f FORMAT, --format=FORMAT
        Use a printf-style format for each number. For example:
        -f "item_%g" produces item_1, item_2, etc.

    --help
        Show a short help message and quit.

EXAMPLES
    seq 5
        Prints: 1  2  3  4  5  (each on its own line)

    seq 3 7
        Prints: 3  4  5  6  7

    seq 0 2 10
        Count from 0 to 10 by 2: 0  2  4  6  8  10

    seq -w 1 10
        Prints: 01  02  03  04  05  06  07  08  09  10

    seq -s ", " 1 5
        Prints: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5

    for i in $(seq 1 5); do echo "Step $i"; done
        Use seq to drive a shell loop.
