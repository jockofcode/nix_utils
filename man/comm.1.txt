NAME
    comm - compare two sorted files and show what they share or don't

WHAT IT DOES
    comm reads two files that are both sorted alphabetically and shows
    three things: lines only in the first file, lines only in the second
    file, and lines that appear in both files.

    Think of it like comparing two sorted lists to find what is unique
    to each list and what they have in common.

    Both files must be sorted before you run comm.

HOW TO USE IT
    comm [options] FILE1 FILE2

    Use - as a filename to read from standard input.

OPTIONS
    -1
        Do not show lines that are only in FILE1.

    -2
        Do not show lines that are only in FILE2.

    -3
        Do not show lines that are in both files.

    --check-order
        Report an error if the input is not in sorted order.

    --nocheck-order
        Do not check whether the input is sorted.

    --output-delimiter=STR
        Use STR instead of a tab to separate the columns.

    --help
        Show a short help message and quit.

EXAMPLES
    comm file1.txt file2.txt
        Show three columns: lines only in file1, lines only in file2,
        and lines in both.

    comm -12 file1.txt file2.txt
        Show only lines that appear in both files.

    comm -3 file1.txt file2.txt
        Show lines that are different between the two files.

    comm -23 file1.txt file2.txt
        Show only lines unique to file1.
