NAME
    sleep - pause for a set amount of time

WHAT IT DOES
    sleep makes the terminal wait for a specified amount of time before
    doing anything else. You can give it a number of seconds, or use
    suffixes for minutes, hours, or days. You can also combine multiple
    times by listing them as separate arguments.

    This is very useful in scripts when you want to wait between steps.

HOW TO USE IT
    sleep NUMBER[SUFFIX]...

    NUMBER can be a whole number or a decimal. SUFFIX can be:
      s  seconds (the default)
      m  minutes
      h  hours
      d  days

OPTIONS
    --help
        Show a short help message and quit.

EXAMPLES
    sleep 5
        Wait 5 seconds.

    sleep 2.5
        Wait 2.5 seconds.

    sleep 1m
        Wait 1 minute.

    sleep 1h 30m
        Wait 1 hour and 30 minutes.

    echo "Starting..."; sleep 3; echo "Done!"
        Print a message, wait 3 seconds, then print another.
