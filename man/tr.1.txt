NAME
    tr - translate or delete characters

WHAT IT DOES
    tr reads from the keyboard (standard input) and replaces or deletes
    individual characters, then writes the result to the screen. Unlike
    most tools, tr never reads files — it always reads from standard
    input, so you pipe data to it.

    Think of it as a character-by-character search and replace.

HOW TO USE IT
    tr [options] SET1 [SET2]

    SET1 is the characters to look for.
    SET2 is what to replace them with (one for one).

    Special notations in sets:
      a-z    all lowercase letters
      A-Z    all uppercase letters
      0-9    all digits
      [:alpha:]  all letters
      [:digit:]  all digits
      [:upper:]  uppercase letters
      [:lower:]  lowercase letters
      [:space:]  whitespace characters

OPTIONS
    -d, --delete
        Delete every character in SET1. Do not use SET2 with this.

    -s, --squeeze-repeats
        When a character in SET1 (or SET2) appears multiple times
        in a row, replace the whole run with just one copy.

    -c, --complement
        Use the opposite of SET1 — match everything NOT in SET1.

    --help
        Show a short help message and quit.

EXAMPLES
    echo "hello" | tr a-z A-Z
        Convert to uppercase: HELLO

    echo "HELLO WORLD" | tr A-Z a-z
        Convert to lowercase: hello world

    echo "hello   world" | tr -s " "
        Squeeze multiple spaces into one: hello world

    echo "a1b2c3" | tr -d 0-9
        Delete all digits: abc

    echo "hello" | tr -d aeiou
        Delete vowels: hll

    cat file.txt | tr "\n" " "
        Replace all newlines with spaces (put everything on one line).
