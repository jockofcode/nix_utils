NAME
    factor - break a number down into its prime factors

WHAT IT DOES
    Every whole number bigger than 1 can be broken down into a set of
    prime numbers that multiply together to make it. Those are called
    prime factors. For example, 12 = 2 × 2 × 3.

    factor does this automatically. You give it a number and it prints
    all the prime factors for that number.

HOW TO USE IT
    factor [options] [NUMBER]...

    You can give numbers as arguments, or type them one per line when
    no arguments are given (press Ctrl+D when done).

OPTIONS
    -h, --exponents
        Show repeated factors using exponent notation.
        For example: 12 = 2^2 3  instead of  12 = 2 2 3.

    --help
        Show a short help message and quit.

EXAMPLES
    factor 12
        Prints: 12: 2 2 3

    factor 100
        Prints: 100: 2 2 5 5

    factor -h 100
        Prints: 100: 2^2 5^2

    factor 17
        Prints: 17: 17   (17 is already a prime number)

    factor 12 100 17
        Factors all three numbers at once.
